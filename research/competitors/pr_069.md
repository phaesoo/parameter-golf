## Three Orthogonal Techniques

### 1. Ternary QAT (Compression Axis)
- Every linear layer uses `BitLinear` with weights constrained to {-1, 0, 1}
- STE forward: `w_q = w + (w_ternary * scale - w).detach()`
- Per-row scale = mean(|w|)
- L1 regularization drives >50% weights to zero → better compression
- **Phased training**: first 20% full BF16, then QAT activates with 0.5x LR reduction
- Packing: 2 bits/value (4 values per byte) + zlib → ~1.7 bits/weight effective
- Result: ~20.7M params in ~3.7MB

### 2. Depth Recurrence (Compute Axis)
- 5 stored blocks, 32 effective layers:
  - 1 prelude block
  - 3 shared blocks × 10 loop iterations = 30 layers
  - 1 coda block
- Per-loop differentiation (cheap, ~88K params total):
  - Unique LayerNorm per iteration
  - Low-rank loop signal (rank-48 down/up projection)
  - Input injection: `(1-sigmoid(alpha))*x + sigmoid(alpha)*x0`
- **Progressive loss** at loops 4 and 7 (weighted 0.3x) for gradient flow

### 3. TTT (Inference Axis)
- Per-document: 30% prefix for 1-step SGD, 70% suffix for scoring
- Only adapts last 2 layers (coda + last shared block)
- lr=1e-4, reset weights between documents

## Novel Ideas

1. **Three genuinely orthogonal axes**: compression (ternary), capacity (recurrence), adaptation (TTT). They don't interfere.
2. **Phased QAT**: full precision warmup → ternary with LR reduction. Avoids instability of ternary from scratch.
3. **Progressive loss as gradient highway**: intermediate losses at loops 4,7 prevent vanishing gradients through 30 recurrent layers.
4. **Input injection with learned sigmoid alpha**: prevents representation collapse in deep recurrence.
5. **Per-loop low-rank signals (rank-48)**: ~88K params total to differentiate 10 loop iterations. Extremely parameter-efficient.
